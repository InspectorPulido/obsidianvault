Your receptionist emails patient records as PDF attachments. Every one sits unencrypted on at least two mail servers, indefinitely. That's a Privacy Act breach waiting to happen.

I built a free tool that fixes this in 30 seconds.

ObsidianVault -- zero-knowledge encrypted file transfer.

Drop a file. Your browser encrypts it with AES-256-GCM. You get a link. The server never sees the unencrypted file. The decryption key lives in the URL fragment -- it never touches our servers.

If we get hacked, they get encrypted blobs. If law enforcement seizes our servers, they get ciphertext. We cannot hand over your files because we never had them.

Free for personal use. Open source. No accounts. No passwords. No software to install.
