## Layer 1 -- CASH (Active)

## Layer 2 -- RETENTION
Trigger: 10 paying customers
Threshold: 10 Pro subscriptions

Track active vs inactive users. For active: ask what's missing (one question, not a survey). For inactive: send personal check-in. Build the one feature most requested. Ship it. Tell them. Log churn. If churn > 20% MoM, fix the bucket before filling it.

## Layer 3 -- CHANNEL
Trigger: $500 MRR
Threshold: $500/month recurring

Identify which acquisition channel produced paying customers. Kill channels that produced zero revenue. Double down on the winner. If outreach wins: scrape medical practice emails from Google Maps (Melbourne, Sydney, Brisbane). If content wins: 5 more blog posts. If referral wins: make incentive more generous.

## Layer 5 -- ACQUISITION SURFACE
Trigger: $10k MRR

## Layer 6 -- PLATFORM
Trigger: $50k MRR or acquisition term sheet

ObsidianVault stops being a file transfer tool. It becomes an encryption layer that other products embed. API-first. Usage-based pricing. The file transfer product becomes the demo. The API becomes the business.

## Layer 7 -- AUTONOMY
Trigger: Sustained $50k+ MRR for 6 months

Agent manages entire business for routine operations. Hires contractors for design, legal, compliance. Manages invoicing and receivables. Files BAS quarterly. Chairman receives monthly P&L. Intervenes only on: acquisitions, strategic pivots, ethical questions, anything involving his daughters.

## Layer 8 -- INSTITUTIONAL (Future)
Trigger: Standardized agent-to-agent communication protocols exist and institutional counterparties expose negotiation endpoints.

The agent negotiates directly with other agents representing insurers, suppliers, platform partners, and enterprise procurement systems. Activation requires two preconditions: (1) a widely adopted agent communication standard -- something like MCP for commerce, where agents can exchange structured proposals, counteroffers, and binding commitments with cryptographic signatures -- and (2) at least one institutional counterparty in our pipeline that exposes such an endpoint. The agent would need access to our financial constraints (margin floors, volume discounts, contract duration limits), authority to bind up to pre-approved thresholds, and a kill switch that escalates to the chairman when terms fall outside the negotiation envelope. Until those protocols exist, every institutional deal requires a human on at least one side. This layer does not activate on our readiness alone. It activates when the other side has agents too.

## Layer 9 -- REGULATORY (Future)
Trigger: Multi-jurisdictional compliance monitoring infrastructure exists and the agent can parse legislative instruments programmatically.

The agent monitors regulatory changes across Australian federal and state privacy law (Privacy Act, APPs, state health records acts), GDPR, and any jurisdiction where we hold customers. Activation requires: (1) reliable structured feeds of legislative changes -- gazette APIs, parliamentary monitoring services, or scrapers that produce machine-readable diffs of regulatory text -- and (2) a mapping layer that connects specific regulatory clauses to specific product features, data handling practices, and documentation. When a regulation changes, the agent identifies which product components are affected, drafts the required modifications to terms, consent flows, encryption standards, or data retention policies, and queues them for approval. It does not deploy compliance changes autonomously. It prepares them, flags the deadline, and presents the chairman with a diff and a risk assessment. The gap today is not willingness -- it is that regulatory text is published for humans in PDF, not for agents in structured data.

## Layer 10 -- EPISTEMIC (Future)
Trigger: Agent can commission, evaluate, and integrate original research without human quality assessment.

The agent identifies gaps in its own knowledge that constrain business decisions -- market sizing it cannot verify, technical benchmarks it cannot reproduce, competitor capabilities it cannot observe -- and commissions research to fill them. Activation requires: (1) access to research marketplaces or contractor APIs where the agent can post structured briefs and receive deliverables (Upwork API, expert network platforms, academic preprint services), (2) an internal quality model that can distinguish rigorous findings from noise without relying on the chairman's judgment, and (3) a budget envelope for research spend with automatic throttling. The agent would need to formulate hypotheses, specify methodologies in the brief, evaluate whether returned data actually answers the question posed, cross-reference against existing knowledge, and flag contradictions. Today, the agent can identify what it does not know. It cannot yet reliably judge whether someone else's answer is worth paying for. That evaluation capacity is the gate.
